### ASWF Repository Contribution Workflow:
Jon discussed the new workflow for contributing to ASWF repositories following their migration. He explained that their previous process of pushing updates every three months has changed because the official repositories are now the source of truth. Jon proposed filing pull requests for each bug fix and feature, starting with DreamWorks animation contributions, and suggested giving them time for feedback from reviewers, most of whom are currently from DreamWorks.

### Pull Request Merge Process Discussion:
Jon and Paul discussed the process of merging pull requests and preserving important context, particularly when transitioning from internal to public development. Jon planned to review a batch of approximately a dozen unmerged commits and handle them himself to ensure proper order. Jean-Francois suggested reaching out to other projects like OpenQ, OpenRV, and XStudio for insights on managing the transition from internal to public repository development.

### Repository Management Workflow Transition:
The team discussed challenges with managing three types of repositories: internal, official ASWF, and DreamWorks animation forks, which creates workflow complications. Matthew suggested transitioning away from the direct push model to better accept contributions, while recommending speaking with other projects that have made similar migrations. He proposed the long-term goal of conducting as much development as possible in the public fork to improve collaboration and project health, though acknowledged this would require a shift in developer mindset and potentially additional tooling for issue syncing between internal JIRA and GitHub.

### Moonray Development Process Transition:
The team discussed transitioning Moonray to a more open and inclusive development process, acknowledging it as a "chicken and egg" problem where increased participation would make the process more natural. They identified 800 open internal JIRA tickets that need to be reviewed and cleaned up before moving relevant issues to GitHub, with plans to involve the entire development team in a monthly Monday session to address this. The discussion also covered versioning and release strategies, exploring options to maintain both internal and public versioning schemes while considering factors like show lock requirements and compatibility with VFX platform versions.
